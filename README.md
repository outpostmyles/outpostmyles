# Myles Schenfield

Self-directed trader and builder working where AI meets markets. Finance degree from SUNY Geneseo, trading equities, options, and futures since 2021. Over the past six months I taught myself full-stack and AI engineering and shipped three production systems for investing, trading, and prediction markets.

Currently focused on prediction markets and fintech, and open to roles where markets, risk, and applied AI meet.

## What I'm building

**[Outpost](https://github.com/outpostmyles/outpost)** · An AI accountability partner for self-directed investors. A portfolio-aware Claude agent, a behavioral coaching layer, and proactive research in a mobile-first PWA. It captures your thesis when you buy and quotes it back when you are about to break your own plan. Live in private beta. Roughly 59,000 lines, 131 API endpoints, 127 test files.

**[Overlay](https://github.com/outpostmyles/overlay)** · A betting research engine that treats prediction-market prices (Polymarket and Kalshi) as the sharp true line, de-vigs them, and measures sportsbooks, props, and an independent model against that line, tracking closing line value to prove edge over time. The thesis: durable edge comes from market structure, not from picking winners.

**[Ballast](https://github.com/outpostmyles/ballast)** · An autonomous AI trading copilot with deterministic safety rails the model can never override. A language model proposes each plan, and pure unit-tested code clamps or vetoes it. Stdlib-only Python, a prop-firm risk engine reproduced to the dollar, and 114 tests that encode the domain rules.

## Background

Finance, SUNY Geneseo. Trading since 2021. I went from not knowing how to build software to shipping these three systems in about six months, and I care a lot about correctness, honest evaluation, and putting non-deterministic models safely near real risk.
